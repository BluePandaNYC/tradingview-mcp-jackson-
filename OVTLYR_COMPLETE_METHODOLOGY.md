## Core Philosophy

**"Markets are people, people are predictable."**

OVTLYR is a behavioral analytics system. It reads crowd fear and greed at the market, sector, and stock level — then trades the contrarian reversion to the mean. When fear is extreme → opportunity to buy. When greed is extreme → exit or wait.

**The 70/30 Rule:**
- 70% weight: Market + Sector layers (must be right FIRST)
- 30% weight: Stock selection (irrelevant if macro conditions are wrong)
- Never focus on the stock until the market and sector are green

**The 90/90/90 Rule (why most traders fail):**
- 90% of traders lose 90% of their money in 90 days
- Root cause: trading without a plan, chasing, no expectancy edge
- Fix: follow the 9 confirmation checklist with strict discipline

## The OVTLYR Nine Confirmation Framework

All 9 must be green for the strongest setup. Check in order — stop if any layer fails.

### LAYER 1: Market Trend
- Chart: SPY daily
- Indicator: 10 EMA, 20 EMA, 50 EMA
- BULLISH: 10 EMA > 20 EMA AND price > 50 EMA
- BEARISH: 10 EMA crosses below 20 EMA OR price below 50 EMA
- Hold position as long as 10 EMA > 20 EMA and price > 50 EMA

### LAYER 2: Market Signal
- OVTLYR proprietary signal on SPY
- States: BUY or SELL
- Must show BUY to proceed
- Current (Jul 2, 2026): BUY at $745.40

### LAYER 3: Market Breadth (Fear & Greed)
- OVTLYR market breadth tool: % of stocks meeting bullish criteria vs its 10 EMA
- Green line = breadth, Red line = 10 EMA of breadth
- GREEN: breadth rising / green line above red line = expanding participation
- RED: breadth falling / green line below red line = shrinking participation
- Check trend direction (rising vs falling), not just the value

### LAYER 4: Sector Fear & Greed Heatmap
- OVTLYR Sector Intelligence Map
- Each sector color-coded: green = greed (bullish), red = fear (contrarian opportunity)
- Select sector where Fear & Greed is turning from fear to greed (recovering)
- Avoid: Healthcare/Pharmaceutical sector explicitly (poor backtested results)

### LAYER 5: Sector Breadth
- % of stocks in chosen sector meeting bullish criteria
- Must show expansion (green line above red line or trending up)
- "Sector relative greed" — compare sectors to find the strongest

### LAYER 6: Stock Trend
- Chart: individual stock daily
- Same 10/20/50 EMA template as market
- Stock must be in uptrend: 10 EMA > 20 EMA, price > 50 EMA

### LAYER 7: Stock Signal
- OVTLYR signal on the individual stock
- Must show BUY
- Check "Stock signal return" — filter stocks by which ones have the best signal return history

### LAYER 8: Stock Fear & Greed (Heatmap)
- Stock-level behavioral sentiment
- Extreme fear → potential entry
- Heatmap stall = exit signal (when greed peaks and momentum slows)

### LAYER 9: OVTLYR Blocks (Order Blocks)
- Price zones where institutional orders cluster/stall
- Used for: entry refinement + exit signals
- Intraday: when price hits a block → switch to 5-minute chart
- Blocks used to exit before price reversal
- Appear in TradingView, thinkorswim, and OVTLYR console

## Options Strategy Rules

### NEVER SELL OPTIONS
OVTLYR is emphatic: **selling options = disastrous for retail traders**  
Cautionary tales taught: OptionsSellers.com (went bankrupt), Long Term Capital Management

### Buying Options (Long Calls Only)
- **Delta: ~80** (deep in the money)
- **Expiration:** Not too close to expiry — choose enough time for the trade to work
- **Open interest:** Must have adequate liquidity
- **Bid/ask spread:** Must be tight (low extrinsic cost)
- **Broker:** Tradier (preferred) or thinkorswim

### Options Rolling (the de-risk technique)
- **When to roll:** When delta drops below ~60 (minimum delta roll point)
- **Best roll:** Roll up and OUT for a credit (free risk reduction)
- **Acceptable roll:** Roll for a small debit (if it makes sense)
- **Effect:** Rolling de-risks the trade; creates zero-risk or near-zero-risk positions
- **Partial profit:** Roll to take 50% off while keeping exposure
- **Championship examples:**
  - KGC: rolled to zero risk
  - PPTA: 27% remaining risk
  - NEM: 6% remaining risk after roll
  - OR: 50% remaining risk

### Position Sizing
- Risk-based: risk only a fixed % of portfolio per trade (6% example in training)
- Volatility-adjusted: higher ATR (Average True Range) = smaller position
- Shares vs options: options provide convexity/asymmetrical edge (more upside, limited downside)
- "Golden rule of leverage" — don't over-leverage; options already provide leverage
- Return-to-risk ratio: must be favorable before entering

## Risk Management

### Stop Loss
- Automatic stop loss in Tradier
- Calculated from ATR: use ATR to determine how much the stock normally moves
- Position sizing = Account size × Risk% ÷ Stop distance

### ATR (Average True Range)
- DeepThought indicator in TradingView shows ATR
- Used to size positions and set stops
- Higher ATR stocks (LUNR, EMBJ examples) → smaller positions
- Lower ATR stocks → can take larger positions

### Sectors to Avoid
- **Healthcare/Pharmaceutical: AVOID** — explicitly taught (USIC content, backtesting confirms poor results)
- Volatile speculative biotech/pharma stocks have unpredictable gaps

### Exit Discipline
- Never let a winner turn into a loser (rolling prevents this)
- "Stop winners from turning into losers" — key psychology lesson
- "3 doors and a lambo theory" — take the guaranteed good outcome, not the gamble
- Roll winning trades to lock in profits while keeping upside

## Backtesting and Expectancy

**Why backtesting matters:**
- Every plan MUST have an expectancy edge
- "Expectancy quarter analogy" — if you flip a biased coin 1000x, math guarantees profit
- Plans without positive expectancy eventually fail even with short-term luck

**Common backtest failures:**
- Over-optimization (curve-fitting to historical data)
- Too small a sample size
- Missing real trading friction (slippage, emotions, gaps)
- "Why most profitable backtests fail in live trading" — a specific OVTLYR lesson

**Valid backtesting:**
- Access OVTLYR backtesting data in the platform
- Test ATR position reduction theory
- Test reduced positions in choppy markets
- Check "Which OVTLYR Plan Works in Stock Market Chop"

**Key metrics:**
- Win rate + frequency + expectancy = complete picture
- Trade frequency matters: too infrequent = not enough sample size
- "Winners vs losers in professional trading" — pro expectancy is not about win rate alone

## Trading Psychology Rules

1. **Stick to your plan** — not to your opinion, feelings, or news
2. **Separate yourself from the outcome** — execute the plan, let the edge play out
3. **Ignore the noise** — financial media, social media, fundamentals
4. **No expectation per trade** — only expectancy over many trades
5. **Stop winners from becoming losers** — roll when profitable; never hold to expiry from deep profit
6. **FOMO kills accounts** — wait for the signal, not the "almost signal"
7. **90/90/90 awareness** — most traders fail because of psychology, not strategy
8. **"Trading in the Zone"** by Mark Douglas — required reading
9. **"Market Wizards"** — interview-based mindset lessons
10. **Don't buy the dip** — wait for OVTLYR signal confirmation (even if it "feels cheap")
11. **Never sell options** — asymmetric risk is against you
